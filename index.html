Our Mission

Redefining Cancer & Neurological Imaging

At the Dwivedi Research Lab, King George's Medical University (KGMU) Lucknow, we pioneer advancements in in-vivo MRI, MR spectroscopy, diffusion MRI modeling, and the integration of advanced imaging with AI and deep learning technologies.

Our focus is on non-invasive imaging solutions for prostate cancer, glioma, kidney tumors, breast cancer, traumatic brain injuries (TBI), and Alzheimer's disease — with a strong emphasis on clinical translation across India.

We embrace a multidisciplinary approach, accentuating personalized medical strategies through the continuous advancement and practical application of MR techniques.

AI · Super-Resolution · Deep Learning

MRI Super-Resolution: FSRCNN & SRDenseNet

We leverage Fast Super-Resolution Convolutional Neural Networks (FSRCNN) and SRDenseNet architectures to enhance the spatial resolution of diffusion MRI images — without expensive high-field hardware. This approach enables near-3T image quality from standard 1.5T scanners, applied across TBI, prostate, and glioma datasets. Combined with NODDI and DKI diffusion models for enhanced tissue microstructure characterization clinically deployable across India.

Physics-Informed Neural Networks · Glioma

PINNs Model for Glioma Diagnostics & Monitoring

Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) embed biological and physical equations — partial differential equations governing tumor growth, diffusion, and metabolism — directly into the neural network loss function. Applied to multi-parametric glioma MRI (ADC, CBV, FLAIR, DCE), PINNs enable biophysically constrained tumor modeling that ensures physiologically plausible predictions even with limited training data.

New Research · Diffusion MRI

Single-Shell to Multi-Shell dMRI Synthesis

Advanced diffusion models — NODDI, CHARMED, MAPMRI — require multi-shell acquisitions unavailable in most clinical protocols. We develop AI synthesis models (GANs, score-based diffusion networks) to generate full multi-shell dMRI data from a standard single-shell clinical acquisition (b=1000, 32 directions).

This enables NODDI-derived ICVF, ODI, and ISOVF metrics; CHARMED restricted diffusion compartments; and MAPMRI microstructure indices — from routine acquisitions with no protocol changes and no additional scan time.

Applications: white matter mapping in TBI, glioma microstructure, Alzheimer's neurodegeneration, retrospective analysis of large clinical dMRI databases.

New Research · Prostate Cancer

Hybrid Multi-dimensional MRI in Prostate Cancer

Hybrid Multi-dimensional MRI (HM-MRI) simultaneously encodes T1, T2, and diffusion contrasts in a single acquisition — enabling comprehensive, co-registered tissue composition maps of the prostate gland unavailable from conventional sequential mpMRI.

Our ongoing work is the first study of its kind in the Asian population — non-invasively characterizing water, lipid, and fibrosis fractions to guide biopsy decisions and address the multi-focality and molecular heterogeneity of prostate cancer.

Directly addresses clinical challenges of PSA overdiagnosis, random biopsy errors, and software fusion biopsy device limitations.
